Why duels beat browsing

Scrolling a list engages the analytical mind — you weigh, compare, second-guess. A forced choice between exactly two names engages something faster and more honest. Parents who play report the same small shock: a name they'd politely shortlisted keeps losing duels, while one they'd dismissed keeps winning. That gap between what you think you like and what you actually choose is the whole value of the game.

The classic way to use it: each partner plays the same style category separately, then you compare top threes. Agreements go straight to the generator to be scored against your surname; disagreements go to the compare tool to be settled with evidence. Tap the ❤️ on any ranked name to keep it in your shortlist.

Frequently asked questions

How does the name battle work?

Two names appear; tap your favourite; the winner stays to face a new challenger. After the rounds finish, names are ranked by how often your instinct picked them.

Can I battle with my surname included?

Yes — add a first name and surname before starting and every duel shows complete combinations, so you're choosing between real full names rather than abstractions.
